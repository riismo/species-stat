Species-Stat

This is a little web app that was created to test a theory that furries are more likely to associate with other furries with the same fursona species.

If that sentence didn't make sense, this project is probably of no interest to you.

This does several things:

  • Requests read-only Twitter access
  • Requests the user input their species
  • Gives the user a survey asking the species (if any) of their Twitter follows
  • Displays a graph of how their responses compare to the overall distribution
  • Gives a sharable link to the user's results

At the end of the survey, you'll get a graph of some data about your responses. You can share your results with a link provided below the graph. If you'd rather not share it, that's fine too: results are not browsable without the user's randomly-generated link.

While identifiable data from individual users will not be shared without permission, aggregate data may be shared after taking reasonable steps to protect the privacy of individual users.

After completing the survey, you can remove the app's Twitter access by going to settings > apps on your Twitter account and selecting "revoke access" for app Species-Stat. The app doesn't need (or use) any access after you've completed the survey, so you can still share your results after doing this.

Note that some tracking prevention software (including Firefox in incognito mode with tracking protection enabled) will break all twitter usericon display on this site. It will still otherwise work.

The survey part doesn't require javascript, but is nicer with it enabled. The graph on the results page requires javascript to display, so you'll miss out on the best part.
